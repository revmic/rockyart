Artist Statement
June 7, 2015

My fascination with the minutia within a grand whole is one of the drives behind my art. I relish in the little observed, hard to find, quiet and lavishly ornate beauties of the small and insignificant; or “unevents” as I see them. I examine the world as if to hold every dust mite and sand grain under a microscope, breaking down the smallest into even smaller. It is from that point that I then create my art.

The circle has become the core symbol in the language of my work. Establishing each circle is to consider a new idea, concept, or perspective in which the world can be understood. My curious and quiet contemplation of my surrounding influences are translated into this unfolding of marks into an open and empty plane. Both in precious metals and on paper, I fill voids and negative spaces with these symbols. This creates a new negative space, more subtle yet more intricately connected, delicately held together by the sinuous veins between each individual mark.

I am also interested in the dichotomy that arises from the ambiguity of uniqueness when individuals are presented as a dense amalgamation. It is hard to discern what draws the eye in: the mark made or the slivers that exist between that texture, or perhaps that juxtaposition of the two. It is this ongoing dialectic that I find in my work and my processes that I believe to be the true work as it exists in the realm of the subjective rather than the objective. This experience of process, the viewer’s engagement in the work, brings the art to fruition. The process is like a circle, without beginning or end but constantly reaching itself and perpetuating a structure of limitations and transcendence.
